"Kids today"

So the saying goes. It can be hard being young. But what's even harder is when our voice isn't heard. We get stereotyped, stigmatized, and targeted by authorities. In many low-income communities, our rights are violated by curfews, stop-and-frisk actions and invasions of privacy that would otherwise be considered unconstitutional if the person were over 18.

We get no say in elections for politicians or for local ordinances that affect us directly. When our behavior is deemed a problem, we are pushed out of school and into the prison system. We don't receive the guidance we need at a crucial point in our lives. And after all this, we get called "apathetic", "uninvolved" and "criminal" by the same authorities who refuse to hear our voice. After enough time, some of us might start to believe it.

Except–it's not true.

Youth are not only the future of our communities, we can also lead change today.

Youth are not only the future of our communities, we are also leaders of today. We have new perspectives which mean new ideas, and we hold the key to solving many of the problems we all face. If society wants to see creative solutions that relate to young people, why not go to the experts—us?

Give us the opportunities to lead and we will rise to the challenge. Organizations like BAY-Peace empower and nurture youth leadership, promote civic involvement, and foster constructive activism.

"Kids today" have plenty to say. Maybe it's time for people to start listening.

BAY-Peace provides youth-led workshops and trainings to give other young people real information about the critical thinking, and understanding among working-class students of color who are the targets of institutions and cultural norms that increase the level of violence in their lives. Located in the San Francisco Bay Area, they are the only group of their kind offering a unique model that brings youth to the forefront of the movement for peace and justice.
